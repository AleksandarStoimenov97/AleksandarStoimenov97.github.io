Pachacamac VR

Pachacamac VR

Since the 1970s, immersive technologies, such as head-mounted displays and digital gloves, have suggested that it is now possible to explore and interact with virtual universes by simulating the physical presence of a user. Long used within a restricted framework due to their complexity and cost of application, these techniques are experiencing a revival thanks to advances in computing and the competitiveness of the video game sector. The inherent qualities of immersive multimedia today offer new perspectives for the representation, management, dissemination and study of cultural heritage.

Although presenting many opportunities, the development of virtual applications for these purposes is still an exceptional event. There is skepticism about its usefulness within the scientific community. Like any other medium, virtual reality is not a neutral mode of representation. It influences the way information is analyzed and interpreted. This coupled with the difficulty to "take a step back" during immersion requires taking into consideration certain methodological aspects to allow its use in a scientific framework.

The following work aims to explore the use of virtual reality for the representation of built heritage. A series of questions are addressed to allow the development of a virtual reality application responding to the specific issues of heritage representation. The product of this work is an explorable and diffusable model which allows to bring a gain of intelligibility on the object studied, in this case, unit B15 of the archaeological site of Pachacamac.
